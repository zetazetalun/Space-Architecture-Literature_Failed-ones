# DESIGN OF COMPOSITE SAMPLING SYSTEM FOR SURFACE SOIL OF EXTRATERRESTRIAL BODIES

**Authors:** Qi-Chen SUN, Zeng ZHAO, Jun-Shan GONG, Zhong-Wang YIN, Xiao-Ming LAI, Hai-Bin YU, Zhong-Xian ZHAO, Chao WANG, and Hui LV

**Journal:** THERMAL SCIENCE
**Year:** 2025
**Volume:** 29, No. 2B
**Pages:** 1267-1272
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.2298/TSCI2502267S

## Abstract
Space resource development has become a frontier topic in international space exploration. The utilization of asteroid resources necessitates extensive regolith mining operations. This paper presents the design and simulation analysis of an extraterrestrial surface regolith composite sampling system. The findings demonstrate that combining spiral drilling and transport with kinetic energy directional excitation is highly effective. It is capable of collecting no less than 500 g within 5 seconds at a rotation speed of 3000 rpm, while maintaining a reaction force below 5 N.

## Introduction
Space resource development is essential for planetary exploration and lunar development. Asteroids are rich in precious metals and rare elements with high utilization value. Current sampling methods (impact splash, drilling after anchoring, gas excitation) have limitations regarding efficiency, energy consumption, or the need for consumables (gas). This research aims to provide technology for large-scale mining to support subsequent extraterrestrial construction and resource development.

## Scheme Design of Sampling System
### Design of Kinetic Energy Directional Sampling
Uses a motor-driven brush on a rotating shaft to impart velocity to particles, directing them into a sample channel. This is effective for microgravity conditions but limited to small particles.

### Design of Auger Drilling Transport Sampling
Employs a spiral flange to cut and transport regolith from deeper levels. Features a small spiral angle and deep groove structure for retention.

### Compound Sampling Principle
Combines the two methods: the drilling tool performs subsurface excavation and crushing, while high-speed brushes direct the particles toward a collection container.

## Simulation and Results
### Collection Efficiency
Simulations with a constant feed speed of 0.01 m/s and varied brush rotation speeds showed:

| Feed speed [ms⁻¹] | Speed [rpm] | Time [s] | Equivalent mass of particles collected [g] |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 0.01 | 1000 | 5 | 579.6 |
| 0.01 | 2000 | 5 | 702.8 |
| 0.01 | 3000 | 5 | 729.4 |

### Reaction Force
The maximum reaction force excited by the brush is 6.6 N in the Z-axis, 0.78 N in the X-axis, and less than 1.3 N in the Y-axis. The peak value remains below 7 N, suitable for low-reaction requirements.

## Conclusion
The composite sampling system integrates spiral drilling with kinetic energy acquisition, showing superior adaptability to varied asteroid surface conditions. It achieves efficient mining (500g in 5s) with minimal reaction forces, supporting large-scale asteroid resource extraction.
