# Multiscale Modeling and Testing of Protein-Bound Regolith and Soils

**Authors:** I. Rosa, M. D. Lepech, D. J. Loftus
**Affiliations:** Stanford University, NASA Ames Research Center
**Venue:** Earth and Space 2018 (ASCE)

## Abstract
Extraterrestrial construction faces challenges due to limited resources and high transport costs. This research focuses on protein-bound soils, a novel composite produced by mixing regolith, water, and biopolymer binders. The resulting material exhibits compressive strength comparable to Portland cement. A multiscale framework is proposed to model fracture properties across four scales: nanoscale (protein mechanics), microscale (particle-binder interactions), mesoscale (crack propagation), and macroscale (bulk mechanical testing).

## Introduction
Surface operations on the Moon and Mars require protection from radiation and meteorite impacts. In situ resource utilization (ISRU) using abundant regolith is essential for sustainable infrastructure. Protein-bound regolith offers a low-energy construction method for roads, landing pads, and shelters.

## Multiscale Framework

### 1. Nanoscale
- **Method:** Nanoindentation.
- **Focus:** Determining the mechanical properties of desiccated protein binders.

### 2. Microscale
- **Method:** MicroCT imaging and Statistically Equivalent Periodic Unit Cells (SEPUC).
- **Focus:** Capturing the geometric arrangement of soil particles, voids, and protein film bridges to obtain homogenized material properties.

### 3. Mesoscale
- **Method:** Rigid Body Spring Model (RBSM).
- **Focus:** Simulating crack initiation and propagation within the homogenized material based on plasticity and yield criteria.

### 4. Macroscale
- **Method:** Triaxial and uniaxial testing with 3D Digital Image Correlation (DIC).
- **Focus:** Experimental validation of numerical models and characterization of failure yield surfaces.

## Comparison of Material Properties

| Property | Portland Cement Mortar | Protein-Bound Regolith |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stiffness (GPa)** | ~22 | ~1.2 |
| **Compressive Strength (MPa)** | ~35 | ~12 |
| **Porosity (%)** | 7 - 20 | 20 - 40 |
| **Binder Weight (%)** | Up to 25 | 5 - 15 |

## Conclusions
The multiscale approach enables the prediction of mechanical behavior for varying mix designs and environments. Future work aims to optimize material compositions for specific load requirements and scale the models to simulate full extraterrestrial structures.
