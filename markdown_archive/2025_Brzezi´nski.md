# Lunar Regolith Improvement by Inducing Interparticle Adhesion with Capillary Forces

**Authors:** Karol Brzeziński, Joanna Julia Sokołowska, Bartłomiej Przybyszewski  
**Publisher:** MDPI (Materials Journal)  
**Published:** 20 May 2025  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.3390/ma18102390

## Abstract
This paper concerns the assessment of the lunar regolith ability to consolidate in the presence of liquid water and develop and sustain cohesion after drying. The research was conducted using the lunar regolith simulant (EAC-1A). Specimens of consolidated material, CCR (Capillary-Consolidated Regolith), were produced and tested for flexural strength. Simple mechanical grinding improved interparticle adhesion with capillary forces, resulting in improved flexural strength (0.123 MPa).

## 1. Introduction
Mankind is on the threshold of the second phase of exploration of the Moon through the Artemis Accords. Creating a stationary 'lunar habitat' is essential for long-term stays. Construction strategies increasingly opt for solutions that maximize the use of local resources (ISRU) to minimize Earth-transported mass. Potential binders discussed include sulfur, polymers, and hydraulic binders like Ordinary Portland Cement (OPC) or geopolymers.

## 2. Materials and Methods
### 2.1. Material
EAC-1A lunar regolith simulant was used, which is a basanite material. Tests compared original LRS to ground LRS (milled in a disc mill).

### 2.2. Specimen Preparation
1. Mix 400g dry LRS with 100g distilled water (0.25 ratio).
2. Pour into custom PTFE-coated steel molds (20x20x80 mm).
3. Air-dry for 2h, then oven-dry at 127 °C for 22h.

## 3. Results and Discussion
Grinding LRS increased the specific surface area (SPA) by over 2.5 times. The un-ground consolidated regolith achieved a mean flexural strength of 0.029 MPa. The ground regolith CCR achieved 0.123 MPa, a significant improvement due to increased contact points and solid bridge formation.

## 4. Conclusions
- Capillary forces can induce interparticle adhesion to create solid materials from loose regolith.
- Finer materials (ground LRS) result in four times higher strength.
- The proposed workflow utilizes water as a reusable technological agent rather than a permanent component, facilitating ISRU-based construction and dust mitigation.
